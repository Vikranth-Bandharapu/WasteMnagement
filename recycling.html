The Recycling Journey

Follow how your discarded items travel from curbside collections to being engineered back into manufacturing loops.

1

Collection

Our fleet collects municipal residential and corporate recycling materials, keeping routes carbon optimized.

2

Smart Sorting

Optical sensors, magnets, and manual sorting isolate paper, plastics, glass, and steel into distinct pure channels.

3

Processing

Sorted items are shredded, washed, melted down, or pulped into raw industrial-grade materials.

4

Re-manufacturing

Pellets, pulps, and metal sheets are supplied to factories to construct brand new consumer goods.

Accepted Materials Guide

Use our interactive checker to understand what items can enter single-stream bins and how they must be prepared.

PET Bottles (No. 1)

Beverage bottles, mouthwash containers, and salad dressing jars.

Prep: Rinse thoroughly and replace plastic cap.
Accepted

HDPE Containers (No. 2)

Milk jugs, laundry detergent canisters, and shampoo bottles.

Prep: Empty completely, rinse, pump-nozzles removed.
Accepted

Cardboard Boxes

Corrugated packaging boxes, shipping boxes, and shoe storage containers.

Prep: Remove tape and staples, flatten completely. Keep dry.
Accepted

Newspapers & Mail

Magazines, office letters, paper bags, envelopes, and flyers.

Prep: Remove plastic wraps, metallic stickers, and window glues.
Accepted

Aluminum & Tin Cans

Soda cans, canned food containers, clean foil wraps.

Prep: Rinse chemical residues off. Flattening is optional.
Accepted

Glass Jars & Bottles

Clear, amber, and green glass condiment jars or drink bottles.

Prep: Rinse and dry. Remove metal lids (recycle lids separately).
Accepted

Tech Electronics

Old phones, computers, power adaptors, printers, circuit boards.

Warning: Cannot go in household bins. Must schedule dropoff.
Special Handling

Batteries

Lithium-ion phone batteries, rechargeable batteries, car batteries.

Warning: Highly reactive. Fire hazard if crushed in regular trucks.
Special Handling

Light Bulbs

Fluorescent tubes, LED lights, mercury vapor bulbs.

Warning: Contains toxic vapor. Take to certified facility.
Special Handling

Understanding the Circular Lifecycle

Refining recycled aluminum, for instance, requires 95% less energy than processing raw bauxite ore. Glass can be recycled infinitely without losing purity or durability.

When plastics sit in municipal dumps, they break down into micro-plastics that permeate agricultural soils and clean drinking aquifers. Composting organic materials prevents anaerobic decay, which releases heavy methane gas.

At EcoWaste, we believe every piece of rubbish has potential. By learning how to segregate items and prep them correctly, you contribute directly to stopping resource depletion.
